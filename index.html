About Me

Hi! I was born and raised in HK and sat the HKDSE myself. I know the exam — the time pressure, the trick questions, the momentum killers. I have been through it all.

I double-majored in Interdisciplinary Studies and Computer Science (minor in Finance). My Interdisciplinary Studies included psychology — which taught me that people learn differently. I adapt to your child's style, not the other way around.

Today I'm a full-time software engineer. I write code, debug complex systems, and think in logic all day. That analytical mindset carries straight into my teaching — I break problems down methodically, find where things break, and fix them. Simplicity is a skill I practise daily, not a job title.

I've been teaching since I was young — classmates, younger relatives, anyone who needed help. It's not a side hustle. It's just what I do.

Adapt to your child. Simple as that.

Learn how they learn

Visual, auditory, reading, or hands-on. I figure it out fast and teach to that.

Train the right muscles

Find weak spots, focus there. Smart practice beats mindless drilling.

Teach thinking, not memorising

If they freeze on an unfamiliar question, I haven't done my job. They need to know how to start.

Speed + accuracy

In the HKDSE, both matter. We train both.

Content is only half the battle.

The other half is how you study.

Spaced repetition

Revisit material at the right intervals so it actually sticks, instead of cramming and forgetting the next week.

Smart note-taking

Most students write notes they can't even read later. I teach a system where notes are clear, organised, and actually useful for revision.

Exam strategy

Knowing which questions to attempt first, how to pace, and when to move on — using the right strategy to attain both speed and accuracy.

Good tutoring isn't just about explaining concepts. It's about giving students the tools to study independently — so they don't need me forever.
